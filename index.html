Lana Del Rey

Lana Del Rey, born Elizabeth Woolridge Grant on June 21, 1985, is an American singer-songwriter known for her stylized cinematic quality and themes that often revolve around melancholia, romance, and glamour. She gained fame with her breakout single "Video Games" in 2011, followed by successful albums like "Born to Die" (2012), "Ultraviolence" (2014), "Lust for Life" (2017), "Norman F***ing Rockwell!" (2019), and "Chemtrails over the Country Club" (2021). Del Rey's work is praised for its unique artistry and her voice's captivating quality. read more
